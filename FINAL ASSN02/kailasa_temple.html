About Kailasa Temple

The Kailasa Temple, located in Ellora, Maharashtra, is an architectural marvel that was carved from a single rock in the 8th century. Dedicated to Lord Shiva, it is part of a larger complex of 34 rock-cut caves. This temple is renowned for its intricate carvings and massive scale, symbolizing the grandeur of ancient Indian craftsmanship.

The temple's construction is often attributed to the Rashtrakuta king Krishna I, and it stands as a testament to the engineering ingenuity of its time. The temple showcases the blend of Dravidian and North Indian architectural styles, making it a unique historical site.

Key Facts About Kailasa Temple

Feature Details
